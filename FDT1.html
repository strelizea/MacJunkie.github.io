"budget" Fractal Design Terra PC Build

By: Matteo Devos 

image:  Reddit

In today's article I will walk you trough the components of an SFX (Small Form Factor) build. The build contains an AMD Ryzen 7600 for the CPU,an AMD Radeon 7900 XT graphics card, 32gb of Kingston FURY beast RAM at 6000 MT/S CL30, a 2TB samsung 980 pro, an ASRock A620i motherboard, a ID-cooling IS-55 (which does actually fit the case, unlike pcpartpicker suggests), and ofcourse a fractal design terra.   

Thisbuild contains no watercooling and is thus not prone to any leaking, but that doesn't make it any easier. Small form factor computers have very little room to manoeuver and lots of parts just wouldn't fit in it. An Ifixit toolkit or alike isn't required, but is always nice to have laying around when disassembling a GPU or case (if you for some reason would want to)

By: Matteo Devos

1. CPU

I chose the AMD Ryzen 7600 for this build because it provides loads of power for not a lot of money. The 7600 has 6 cores, and most apps currently only use 2-3, so this will get you going for a while. Like a CPU usually has, it has double the threads, making it 12 threads. It has a clock of 3.GHz and a boost clock of 5.1GHz, which for normal use is plenty and powers trough your tasks. This CPU costs around 200 dollarson the sites I searched trough.   

2. RAM

Let's be real here, the minimum for gaming in 2024 is 16gb. Throw in a bigger game, a youtube video running on chrome with a few extensions and you'll need 32gb. Kingston FURY beast has a speed of 6000 MT/S at CL30, in other words: fast, very fast. Yes, you could get 6400 MT/S speed, but 99% of people won't notice the difference between 6000 and 6400 MT/.

3. GPU

 I chose the AMD Radeon 7900 XT for this build. It beats the Nvidia 4070 Ti in raw power and vram, but lacks on ray tracin. The major advantage it has is its 24gb vram, making it more adequate for games like flight simulator. A good middleground between them is the 4070 Ti Super, at a slightly lower price, 8gb Vram less (16gb instead of 24gb), but has CUDA cores and better ray tracing. Take any you want, but here I'm going for the 7900 XT.
